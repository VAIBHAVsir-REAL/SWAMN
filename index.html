The Crisis

Oceans drowning in plastic

  • 14M Metric tonnes of plastic enter our oceans every single year, threatening marine life and human food security.
  • 1.8T Plastic pieces larger than 0.5mm float in the Great Pacific Garbage Patch alone — 100,000 metric tonnes total.
  • 2040 Without intervention, annual ocean plastic input could reach 23–37 million metric tonnes per year by 2040.
  • 100s Of species including fish, seabirds, and turtles mistake plastic for food — leading to injuries, starvation, and death.
System Architecture

Three layers of intelligence

Swamn operates as a three-layer networked system, combining edge computing, AI inference, and autonomous control for continuous marine cleanup.

👁️
Perception Layer
Pi Camera Module v2 streams live frames to a ResNet18 + KNN inference pipeline. Each frame is classified as GARBAGE or CLEAR every ~500ms.
ModelResNet18 + KNN
Camera8MP, 1080p@30fps
Inference Rate~2 Hz
🧠
Control Layer
Raspberry Pi 5 acts as the central brain, sending HTTP commands to motor controllers and the belt-net mechanism based on AI decisions.
ComputeRPi 5, 4GB RAM
Motor MCUESP32 WROOM-32
GPS MCUESP8266 @ 1Hz
⚡
Power Layer
A 12V SLA battery powers all systems. When voltage drops below 11.5V, Swamn autonomously returns to its solar docking station to recharge.
Battery12V 7Ah SLA
Solar Panel10W Mono
Runtime5.5 Hours
AI Pipeline

From pixel to action

📸
Capture
Pi Camera streams 1080p frames at 30fps to the Raspberry Pi 5
🔄
Preprocess
Frames resized, cropped & normalized. Sent to inference server via HTTP
🤖
Classify
ResNet18 extracts features; KNN classifies as GARBAGE or CLEAR
🧭
Navigate
Pi 5 sends motor commands — approach, scan or return to dock
🪣
Collect
Belt-net conveyor scoops debris in ~11.4 seconds average

Roadmap

What comes next

The current prototype is just the beginning. Future iterations of Swamn will expand capability, environment, and scale.

01
Submerged Debris Detection
Expanding the sensing stack with sonar and underwater cameras to detect and recover plastics below the surface, targeting microplastic accumulation zones.
02
Turbulent Open-Water Testing
Moving beyond controlled tank conditions to real coastal environments — harbors, rivers, and estuaries — with wave-compensation algorithms and ruggedized hull.
03
Multi-Robot Coordination
Developing swarm intelligence for fleets of Swamn units to coordinate large-scale cleanup operations, share detection data, and optimize coverage in real time.
